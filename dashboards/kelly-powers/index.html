▶1 pages returning 404 errors 1 HIGH These pages return a 404 Not Found status. Search engines will deindex them and users hitting these URLs get a dead end. Set up 301 redirects to the most relevant live page. If the content was removed intentionally, ensure proper 410 Gone status and update internal links.
▶2 pages with 3xx redirects 2 MEDIUM Redirect chains waste crawl budget and dilute link equity. Too many redirects slow down page discovery. Update internal links to point directly to the final destination URL. Remove unnecessary redirect hops. Consolidate redirect chains to single 301s.
▶9 pages with titles below 30 characters 9 LOW Very short titles miss the opportunity to include relevant keywords and may not clearly describe the page content. Expand titles to include the primary keyword, a modifier, and the brand name where appropriate.
▶13 pages with missing meta descriptions 13 MEDIUM Missing meta descriptions mean Google auto-generates the snippet. This often results in less compelling search result listings. Write unique meta descriptions (70-155 characters) for each page that include the target keyword and a clear call to action.
▶4 pages with meta descriptions over 155 characters 4 LOW Meta descriptions over 155 characters get truncated in search results, cutting off your message mid-sentence. Trim meta descriptions to 155 characters or fewer. Front-load the most important information and CTA.
▶17 pages with missing H1 tags 17 HIGH The H1 tag is the primary heading signal for search engines. Missing H1s weaken topical relevance and hurt rankings. Add a single, unique H1 tag to each page that includes the primary keyword and clearly describes the page topic.
▶15 pages with missing canonical tags 15 HIGH Without canonical tags, search engines may index duplicate versions of these pages, splitting ranking signals. Add a self-referencing canonical tag to each page. For duplicate content, point the canonical to the preferred version.
▶1 pages with thin / low content 1 MEDIUM Pages with very little content provide minimal value to users and are unlikely to rank. Google may flag these as low-quality. Expand content to at least 300 words with useful, unique information. If the page serves no purpose, consider consolidating or removing it.
